I'm low-passing my electret mic before the ADC at 1 kHz. With R = 10k and C = 15n, what's the actual cutoff and is the loading on the next stage going to be a problem?

eeencyclopedia · 2:14 pm

The −3 dB cutoff of a single-pole RC low-pass is fc = 1/(2πRC). With R = 10kΩ and C = 15nF:

Step 1 · cutoff frequency
fc = 1 / (2π · 10×10³ · 15×10⁻⁹)
   = 1 / (2π · 1.5×10⁻⁴)
   = 1061.03 Hz ≈ 1.06 kHz

You'll get a touch above the textbook 1.0 kHz target — fine for voice. Loading matters because the output impedance is dominated by R: at DC Zout = 10 kΩ, dropping toward the cap reactance above fc.

Rule of thumb: keep the next stage's input impedance ≥ 10× your filter R. If your ADC is direct (no buffer), check its Rs,max in the datasheet — most SAR ADCs want Rs < 1–3kΩ for full-rate sampling. If you're sampling at > ~50 kSPS, add a unity-gain buffer between filter and ADC.

Sample rate is 16 kHz on an SAMD21. Buffer or no buffer?

eeencyclopedia · 2:16 pm

SAMD21 SAR sampling at 16 kHz — you're well below the impedance pain point. Datasheet calls out a max source impedance of 3.5 kΩ for full accuracy at the highest sampling rates; at 16 kHz with the default sample time you have margin. Skip the buffer.

One catch: 10kΩ + 15nF settles in a few RC ≈ 150µs before reaching ½ LSB. At 16 kHz your sample window is 62.5 µs, so the ADC's S/H cap will see step changes per sample. Increase SAMPLEN in the SAMD21 ADC config to give it 8–16 ADC clocks of acquisition, or drop R to 1k and bump C to 150n.
