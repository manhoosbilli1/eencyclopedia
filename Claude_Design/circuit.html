What this circuit does

A first-order passive low-pass between an audio source and the ADC. R1 (10kΩ) and C1 (15 nF) set fc = 1/(2πRC) ≈ 1.06 kHz, attenuating any signal content above the voice band by 6 dB/octave. Output impedance at DC is R1; at high frequency it drops toward the C1 reactance, so the downstream stage should be high-impedance (≥ 100kΩ) to avoid loading.

Topology is the textbook RC LP — no opamp, no buffer. Suitable for line-level signals where loss of a few dB above 1 kHz is intentional. Not suitable as an anti-aliasing filter for a fast ADC; bump fc and switch to an active filter for that.
