Closest E24 value: 240Ω · resulting If ≈ 12.08 mA · power dissipated in R: 35 mW. Use a 1/8 W resistor.
1.Apply KVL around the loop
→Vs = Vf + I·R
2.Solve for R
→R = (Vs − Vf) / I
→R = (5.0 − 2.1) / 0.012
→R = 2.9 / 0.012 = 241.67 Ω
3.Round up to nearest E24 → 240 Ω
→P = I²·R = (0.012)²·240 = 0.0346 W
e

Why round up? A higher R means slightly less current — safer for the LED's lifetime. The 240Ω choice gives 12.08 mA vs your 12 mA target — within 1%. Want me to recompute for a 3.3V rail or a chain of 3 LEDs?
