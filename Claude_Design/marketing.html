How it works

Drop in a .kicad_sch. Get an indexed, queryable circuit.

No workflow change. Same file you save in KiCad. eencyclopedia handles the rest — parse, render, summarise, embed, store.

01 · UPLOAD

Drop a schematic

Single .kicad_sch file. Up to 5 components in V0 (cap rises with the platform).

02 · PARSE & RENDER

Render to SVG

S-exp → AST → clean schematic SVG. Every net and designator becomes a hover target.

03 · SUMMARISE

AI extracts intent

Topology, rails, key components, design notes. Stored as structured JSON — not just prose.

04 · RECALL

Ask it anything

Natural-language queries hit a hybrid (FTS + vector) index. Answers cite the exact circuit.

FAQ

Honest answers.

Who is this for?
Working electronics engineers, EE students, and serious hobbyists who already use KiCad and have a folder of schematics they can't keep track of. If you've ever redrawn a circuit because you couldn't find the original, this is for you.
Does the AI train on my circuits?
No. Your uploads are embedded for retrieval inside your account. We don't fine-tune any model on your data. The encyclopedia improves as the public knowledge base grows — datasheets, textbooks, and circuits people choose to share publicly.
What file formats?
.kicad_sch in V0. We parse the S-expression, normalise to a canonical subset, and render to SVG with hover hooks. Other EDA tools are on the roadmap once we're confident the KiCad path is rock solid.
How big can a circuit be?
V0 caps at 5 components per circuit so the AI summary stays accurate. The cap rises to 10 in V1 and 20 in V2 as the parser, render pipeline, and prompts harden.
Is this fine-tuned?
Not yet — and we're upfront about it. V0 is a strong system prompt + tool use (calculators, RAG, parts lookup) over a frontier model. Once we have a few thousand thumbs-up signals, fine-tuning a smaller model on the corpus is on the roadmap.
Pricing?
Free during closed beta. Paid tiers ship at public launch — Free (10 ops/mo, public circuits), Pro ($12/mo, 100 ops, private circuits), Pro+ ($29/mo, 400 ops, API + Opus access). Solo-built and bootstrapped, so pricing reflects real cost.
